An honest definition

A retainer is one specific thing. It's worth being clear what it isn't.

Most ongoing consultancy products are either underpriced and over-promised, or so vague that you never feel like you got your money's worth. The retainer is designed around a specific, predictable scope.

What it is

  • A second pair of expert eyes on your operation, on a predictable monthly rhythm
  • Early warning detection — problems caught when they're small, not crisis-sized
  • A sounding board for operational decisions where you'd benefit from outside input
  • Light-touch external accountability — knowing the monthly review is coming keeps the improvement cadence honest

What it isn't

  • Implementation work. If something needs doing, the retainer identifies it; you (or transformation) deliver it.
  • Crisis response. If your operation is genuinely broken, you need an audit or transformation engagement, not a retainer.
  • Unlimited advisory. The retainer covers reasonable ad-hoc questions, not on-demand consulting at no extra cost.
  • A long-term lock-in. 30-days notice cancellation, no minimum term, no exit fees. If it's not valuable, you stop.

Honest fit

A retainer works for specific operational situations.

Right fit if you…

  • Have completed a PainKiller audit or transformation programme and want to maintain the gains
  • Have a relatively stable operation but want monthly external perspective to catch drift early
  • Want a sounding board for operational decisions without hiring a full-time consultant
  • Are an operations director who'd value structured external accountability for the improvement cadence
  • Have your own KPI dashboard and want a second set of eyes reviewing it monthly

Worth knowing before enquiring

  • If your operation is genuinely broken right now, the retainer is the wrong product — it's maintenance, not repair. Start with an audit (£950)
  • If you want hands-on implementation work, the transformation programme is what you need
  • If you only need a single piece of advisory, the Rapid-Resolve session (£297) is much better value than a retainer
  • If you don't run regular KPI tracking, the monthly review will have less to work with — get the basics in place first
If you haven't yet had your operation independently assessed, the audit (£950) is the natural first step. A retainer is much more useful once we have a shared baseline of what your operation looks like.
