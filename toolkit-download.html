Getting the most out of these

Three things worth knowing before you start.

01
Adapt before you adopt.

Every operation is different. Open each file, change the column headers to match your terminology, and adjust the example data to reflect your typical numbers. The structure of the templates is what matters — the specific values are placeholders.

02
Roll out one at a time.

Don't try to implement all five at once. Start with the one that maps onto your most visible problem. If overtime is the issue, start with the Labour Tracker. If accuracy is the issue, start with the KPI Dashboard. Get one embedded before adding another.

03
Track for at least four weeks.

Single-week data is noise. Patterns only emerge over 3–4 weeks of consistent tracking — which is also long enough that the templates become habit rather than admin. If you're going to drop the tracking after one week, don't start.

04
Look for systematic, not individual.

When the numbers reveal a pattern, the cause is almost always systematic — process, layout, scheduling, handover — not the people executing. Resist the urge to performance-manage individuals based on variance. The data is for finding causes, not for managing performance.
