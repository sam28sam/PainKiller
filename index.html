The Problem

High labour costs are rarely the real problem.

When overtime keeps climbing and output doesn't follow, it feels like a headcount issue. It almost never is.

What looks like a people problem is usually process waste — poor flow, wasted movement, invisible bottlenecks repeating themselves across every shift. The kind of thing that only becomes visible when someone stands on the floor and looks for it properly.

Most operations managers feel the symptoms. Very few have the time — or the outside perspective — to find the actual cause.

  • Overtime that never fully clears, no matter how many people you add
  • Pick rates that plateau regardless of training or incentives
  • Goods-in backlogs that ripple into every other part of the operation
  • Error rates you've quietly accepted as normal
  • Bottlenecks that appear to move but never disappear
  • KPIs that look acceptable on paper but feel wrong on the floor
  • A site that runs fine — until it doesn't, and no one's sure why

Results

What this work has delivered.

17%
Reduction in Overtime
Multi-shift operation · ~80 staff

Overtime was being driven by planning gaps and handover failures between shifts — not by genuine volume. Shift structure and workflow were restructured. The overtime requirement fell 17% within eight weeks.

22%
Increase in Pick Rate
Retail warehouse · e-commerce fulfilment

Poor product slotting and wasted travel time were the root cause — not picker performance. Picking logic was restructured and movement waste reduced. Pick rate increased 22% with the same headcount.

28%
Faster Goods-In to Shelf
FMCG operation · high-throughput site

Delays across intake, checking, and putaway were creating downstream drag. The intake flow was redesigned to remove waiting time at the key handoff points. Goods reached shelf 28% faster within six weeks.

All results anonymised. Sector, site size, and context available on request for qualifying enquiries.
