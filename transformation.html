What this actually is

A clear definition matters here — because most "transformation consulting" isn't.

There's a lot of warehouse consultancy that calls itself transformation. Most of it is decks, frameworks, and quarterly reviews. This isn't that. The structure below makes the difference explicit.

What you get

  • Sam on the floor 2–3 days per week, for 12 weeks
  • Direct execution of audit findings — restructured shifts, reslotted product, redesigned processes
  • Coaching of your supervisors and team leaders, embedded in their actual work
  • Weekly 1-hour review call with you and your leadership, focused on what's changing and what's not
  • Monthly written progress report — what's been implemented, what's pending, what's blocked, with named owners
  • Phone access between site days for urgent issues
  • A handover document at the end so your team owns the operating model going forward

What you don't get

  • Decks. There are no PowerPoint presentations in this engagement.
  • Big-firm jargon, frameworks-with-acronyms, or quarterly reviews that don't change anything
  • Detached recommendations. Sam isn't writing reports for you to action later — he's doing the work alongside your team
  • An offshored implementation team. This is Sam, in your warehouse, for three months
  • A subscription that drifts. The programme runs 90 days and ends. Continuation, if useful, becomes a retainer
The 90-day shape

Three phases. Each with a clear purpose, deliverable, and end-state.

The shape of the engagement is the same regardless of starting condition. The work within each phase is tailored to your specific findings.

Days 1–30 · Stabilise

Stop the bleeding. Fix the visible damage first.

Quick wins from the audit get executed. Shift handovers tightened. Critical bottlenecks unblocked. KPI dashboards rebuilt so leadership can see what's actually happening. Most operations see measurable improvement inside the first 30 days — not because the deep work is done, but because the obvious dysfunction is removed.

Phase 1 deliverables
  • Quick wins implemented
  • Live KPI dashboard operational
  • Stabilisation report delivered to leadership
Days 31–60 · Implement

The deep work. Restructure what's structurally wrong.

The bigger items from the audit are tackled — slotting redesigned, shift structures revised, goods-in flow rebuilt. This is the most intensive phase because it's the work that takes weeks to implement, not days. Sam is on-site more often during this phase. The operation may feel less smooth before it feels better.

Phase 2 deliverables
  • Core process redesigns live
  • Supervisor capability uplifted through 1-on-1 coaching
  • Mid-engagement review with leadership
Days 61–90 · Embed

Make it stick. Hand it over to your team.

The final phase is about embedding the new operating model so it survives Sam leaving. Documentation is built. Supervisors are taking ownership without prompting. The improvement cadence is running on its own. By Day 90, your team owns the operation, and the only Sam-shaped hole left is the strategic one — which is what the retainer is for, if useful.

Phase 3 deliverables
  • Process documentation completed
  • Handover pack to operations leadership
  • Final review and continuation recommendations

Honest fit

This is a serious commitment. Make sure it's the right one.

Right fit if you…

  • Have completed an on-site audit and want the findings actually implemented, not just filed
  • Are an operations director or MD with the authority to unlock decisions during the engagement
  • Run a UK or accessible European warehouse operation with 30+ staff
  • Have a clear sponsor at senior level who'll champion the work internally
  • Are willing to share operational data, financial context, and political reality — the engagement only works on honesty
  • Are committed to a 90-day engagement, not just an exploration

Worth knowing before enquiring

  • If you haven't yet identified what needs transforming, the audit comes first — £950 is a much smaller commitment than £13,500
  • If you're looking for ongoing light-touch support (monthly review, KPI oversight, ad-hoc advisory), the retainer is the right product, not this
  • If you can't free up internal capacity to work alongside Sam, the engagement won't land — transformation requires your team's involvement, not just Sam's
  • If the budget needs business-case construction, that's fine — but be honest about that in your enquiry so we don't build false expectations
If you're not yet sure whether transformation is the right next step, the on-site audit (£950) is the natural first move. Or book a free 20-minute call to talk through your situation.
